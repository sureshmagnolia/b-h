PHANEROGAMS (SEED-BEARING PLANTS)
DICOTYLEDONS
Two cotyledons, reticulate venation, pentamerous flowers, ringed vascular bundles
POLYPETALAE
Corolla of free distinct petals
THALAMIFLORAE — Sepals distinct/free. Petals and stamens hypogynous (inserted on thalamus, below superior ovary).
RANALES
Many stamens, apocarpous carpels
1. RANUNCULACEAE
Herbs with alternate dissected leaves. Flowers bisexual, parts free and spirally arranged, stamens numerous, apocarpous.
2. DILLENIACEAE
Shrubs, trees, or climbers with alternate simple leaves. Flowers with numerous stamens, carpels separate with arillate seeds.
3. CALYCANTHACEAE
Aromatic shrubs with opposite leaves. Flowers with numerous tepals, stamens, and free carpels inside a hollow receptacle.
4. MAGNOLIACEAE
Aromatic trees/shrubs with stipules. Showy flowers with spirally arranged perianth, numerous stamens and carpels on an elongated receptacle.
5. ANONACEAE
Aromatic trees or shrubs. Flowers trimerous, sepals 3, petals 6, numerous stamens, apocarpous carpels forming aggregate fruit.
6. MENISPERMACEAE
Dioecious twining vines. Leaves alternate, palmately veined. Flowers small, unisexual, trimerous, drupaceous fruits with curved seeds.
7. BERBERIDACEAE
Herbs or shrubs. Flowers trimerous, stamens opening by valves, single carpel with basal placentation.
8. NYMPHAEACEAE
Aquatic herbs with floating peltate leaves. Flowers large, showy, solitary, numerous petals and stamens on a fleshy disc.
PARIETALES
Parietal placentation
9. SARRACENIACEAE
Carnivorous herbs with pitcher-shaped leaves. Flowers large, nodding, style forming a broad umbrella-like structure.
10. PAPAVERACEAE
Herbs with milky/colored latex. Sepals 2-3 caducous, petals 4, stamens numerous, parietal placentation.
11. CRUCIFERAE
Herbs with watery biting sap. Flowers cruciform, tetradynamous stamens, siliqua fruit with false septum.
12. CAPPARIDEAE
Herbs/shrubs with alternate leaves. Flowers with prominent gynophore or androgynophore, parietal placentation.
13. RESEDACEAE
Herbs with alternate leaves. Inflorescence a spike or raceme, flowers zygomorphic, stamens on an eccentric disc.
14. CISTINEAE
Shrubs or herbs. Flowers regular, showy, sepals 3-5 convolute, stamens numerous, parietal placentation.
15. VIOLARIEAE
Herbs or shrubs, alternate stipulate leaves. Flowers zygomorphic, lower petal spurred, stamens 5 forming a ring around ovary.
16. CANELLACEAE
Aromatic trees with pellucid-dotted leaves. Flowers regular, stamens fused into a tube, parietal placentation.
17. BIXINEAE
Trees or shrubs with alternate leaves. Flowers regular, stamens numerous, capsule fruit with seeds often surrounded by colored pulp.
POLYGALYNEAE
Irregular flowers, bilocular ovary
18. PITTOSPOREAE
Trees/shrubs with resin ducts. Leaves alternate, entire. Flowers regular, pentamerous, parietal/axile placentation.
19. TREMANDREAE
Xerophytic shrubs with heath-like habit. Flowers solitary, regular, anthers opening by terminal pores, seeds arillate.
20. POLYGALAE
Herbs or shrubs. Zygomorphic papilionaceous-like flowers, 5 sepals (2 large wing-like), 8 stamens fused in a split tube.
21. VOCHYSIACEAE
Trees/shrubs with resinous sap. Flowers irregular, one calyx lobe spurred, single stamen, capsule or winged samara fruit.
CARYOPHYLLINIAE
Opposite leaves, free-central placentation
22. FRANKENIACEAE
Halophytic salt-glandular herbs. Leaves opposite, small. Flowers regular, petals clawed, parietal placentation.
23. CARYOPHYLLEAE
Herbs with swollen nodes, opposite entire leaves. Flowers regular, clawed petals, free-central placentation.
24. PORTULACEAE
Succulent herbs with opposite or alternate leaves. Flowers regular, sepals 2, stamens opposite petals, capsule opening by a lid.
25. TAMARISCINEAE
Shrubs or trees with scale-like leaves. Flowers small in spikes, petals free, seeds with a tuft of hairs.
GUTTIFERALES
Opposite leaves, numerous stamens
26. ELATINEAE
Small aquatic or moisture-loving herbs. Leaves opposite, stipulate. Flowers small, axillary, axile placentation.
27. HYPERICINEAE
Herbs or shrubs with gland-dotted opposite leaves. Flowers yellow, stamens numerous and united in 3-5 bundles.
28. GUTTIFERAE
Trees/shrubs with resinous yellow sap. Flowers regular, stamens numerous and clustered, seeds without endosperm.
29. TERNSTROEMIACEAE
Trees or shrubs. Alternate simple leaves without stipules. Flowers solitary, regular, stamens numerous.
30. DIPTEROCARPEAE
Resinous forest trees. Flowers sweet-scented, calyx lobes developing into long wings on the fruit.
31. CHLENACEAE
Trees/shrubs endemic to Madagascar. Flowers showy, surrounded by an involucre, stamens numerous, fruit a capsule.
MALVALES
Stellate hairs, monadelphous stamens
32. MALVACEAE
Herbs/shrubs with stellate hairs, alternate stipulate leaves. Flowers regular, stamens fused in a monadelphous tube, anthers 1-loculed.
33. STERCULIACEAE
Herbs/trees with stellate hairs. Alternate simple/compound leaves. Flowers regular, monadelphous stamens, anthers 2-loculed.
34. TILIACEAE
Trees/shrubs with mucilage. Leaves alternate, stipulate. Flowers regular, stamens numerous and free or in bundles.
DISCIFLORAE — Sepals & petals free. Stamens hypogynous, inserted on/around a prominent nectar disc at base of superior ovary.
GERANIALES
Ovary multi-locular, nectary disc present
35. LINEAE
Herbs or shrubs. Leaves alternate, entire. Flowers regular, pentamerous, stamens fused at base, ovary superior.
36. HUMIRIACEAE
Balsamiferous trees/shrubs. Leaves alternate. Flowers regular, stamens 10-30 with expanded connectives, drupe fruit.
37. MALPIGHIACEAE
Woody climbers with T-shaped hairs. Flowers clawed, calyx with prominent glands, winged samara fruits.
38. ZYGOPHYLLEAE
Succulent herbs/shrubs of arid zones. Leaves opposite, pinnate. Flowers regular, stamens with basal scales.
39. GERANIACEAE
Herbs with glandular hairs. Alternate lobed leaves. Flowers regular, pentamerous, fruit a schizocarp with elastic awns.
40. RUTACEAE
Aromatic plants with pellucid gland-dotted leaves. Flowers regular, stamens on a prominent nectar disc.
41. SIMARUBEAE
Trees or shrubs with bitter bark. Leaves alternate, pinnate. Flowers small, unisexual or bisexual, disc present.
42. OCHNACEAE
Trees or shrubs with shiny serrate leaves. Flowers yellow, stamens numerous, anthers opening by pores, torus enlarged in fruit.
43. BURSERACEAE
Resinous trees/shrubs with aromatic balsam. Leaves alternate, pinnate. Flowers small, nectar disc prominent.
44. MELIACEAE
Trees/shrubs with pinnate exstipulate leaves. Flowers in panicles, stamens fused into a characteristic tube.
45. CHAILLETIACEAE
Shrubs or trees. Leaves alternate, entire. Flowers small, petals bifid, ovary superior, drupe fruit.
OLACALES
Disc cushion-like, superior ovary
46. OLACINEAE
Trees or shrubs, often hemiparasitic. Flowers small, calyx enlarging in fruit, disc cup-shaped.
47. ILICINEAE
Evergreen trees or shrubs. Leaves alternate, simple. Flowers small, unisexual, drupe with multiple pyrenes.
48. CYRILLACEAE
Shrubs or small trees. Leaves alternate, simple, exstipulate. Flowers small in racemes, stamens 5 or 10, fruit a capsule or drupe.
CELESTRALES
Disc prominent, stamens alternate with petals
49. CELASTRINEAE
Shrubs or trees. Leaves opposite or alternate. Flowers small, regular, disc flat and adnate to calyx, stamens alternate with petals.
50. STACKHOUSIEAE
Herbs with alternate simple leaves. Flowers in spikes, corolla tubular with free claws, fruit of 3-5 eremicos.
51. RHAMNEAE
Trees/shrubs, often thorny. Leaves simple, stipules often modified into spines. Stamens opposite the small hooded petals.
52. AMPELIDEAE
Climbing plants with tendrils opposite the leaves. Flowers small, stamens opposite petals, fruit a berry.
SAPINDALES
Compound leaves, disc prominent
53. SAPINDACEAE
Trees/shrubs, compound leaves. Flowers small, irregular, nectar disc present. Seeds often arillate.
54. SABIACEAE
Trees or shrubs. Leaves alternate, simple or pinnate. Flowers irregular, stamens opposite petals, drupe fruit.
55. ANACARDIACEAE
Trees/shrubs with resinous/milky sap. Flowers small, disc present, single carpel fertile, drupe fruit.
POTIUS GENERA ANOMALA

Shrubs with angled branchlets and opposite leaves. Flowers small, petals fleshy and accrescent in fruit.

Herbs or shrubs. Leaves alternate or opposite. Flowers regular, perigynous, carpels 2 fused at base, styles free.

64. HAMAMELIDEAE
Shrubs or trees with stellate hairs. Leaves alternate. Flowers regular or apetalous, ovary half-inferior.
65. BRUNIACEAE

66. HALORAGEAE

MYRTALES

Herbs, shrubs, or trees with opposite leaves. Flowers regular, calyx tube persistent, crinkled petals.

Herbs or shrubs. Flowers regular, 4-merous, hypanthium tube present, inferior ovary.

Herbs or shrubs. Leaves alternate, serrate. Flowers regular, petals yellow and inserted on calyx tube, capsule fruit.

Succulent herbs with asymmetric leaves. Flowers unisexual, tepals colorful, capsule fruit 3-winged.

Flowers in umbels, inferior ovary

Herbs with equitant leaves. Flowers zygomorphic, perianth of 2 large tepals, single stamen.
183. XYRIDEAE
Tufted rush-like herbs. Flowers yellow in dense spikes with imbricate bracts, capsule fruit.
184. MAYACEAE
Small moss-like aquatic herbs. Leaves small, linear. Flowers regular, trimerous, superior ovary.
185. COMMELINACEAE
Succulent herbs, closed leaf sheaths. Flowers trimerous, sepals and petals distinct, superior ovary.
186. RAPATEACEAE
Perennial herbs. Leaves in a rosette. Inflorescence head-like on a long scape, capsule fruit.
CALYCINAE
Perianth sepaloid or dry (green/brown), ovary superior.
187. FLAGELLARIEAE
Climbers with leaf tips modified into tendrils. Flowers small, regular, drupe fruit.
188. JUNCACEAE
Rush-like tufted herbs. Leaves linear or reduced. Flowers small, dry and chaffy, superior ovary.
189. PALMAE
Woody plants with unbranched trunk and terminal crown of pinnate/palmate leaves. Berry or drupe fruit.
NUDIFLORAE
Perianth absent or reduced to small scales/bristles, ovary superior.
190. PANDANEAE
Shrubs or trees with stilt roots, leaves linear and spiny-margined in spirals. Dioecious, syncarp fruit.
191. CYCLANTHACEAE
Palm-like or climbing herbs. Leaves deeply bifid. Inflorescence spadix with deciduous spathes.
192. TYPHACEAE
Marsh herbs. Leaves linear. Inflorescence a dense terminal cylindrical spike of unisexual flowers.
193. AROIDEAE
Tuberous herbs, leaves broad. Spadix inflorescence enclosed by a spathe, berries.
194. LEMNACEAE
Tiny floating thalloid aquatics without distinct stem or leaves. Flowers minute, unisexual.
APOCARPAE
Ovary superior, composed of free carpels (apocarpous).
195. TRIURIDEAE
Small saprophytic herbs without chlorophyll. Flowers unisexual, carpels numerous and free.
196. ALISMACEAE
Marsh or aquatic herbs. Leaves long-petioled. Flowers regular, sepals 3 green, petals 3 white, carpels numerous.
197. NAIADACEAE
Submerged marine or freshwater herbs. Flowers tiny, unisexual, apetalous.
GLUMACEAE
Flowers in spikelets, enclosed by scale-like bracts (glumes), perianth reduced/chaffy.
198. ERIOCAULEAE
Tufted herbs. Inflorescence a small dense globose head of tiny unisexual flowers with scarious bracts.
199. CENTROLEPIDEAE
Small tufted moss-like herbs. Flowers tiny, enclosed in scarious bracts.
200. RESTIACEAE
Rush-like herbs with tufted stems. Leaves reduced to sheaths. Dioecious, dry chaffy perianth.
201. CYPERACEAE
Sedges, solid 3-angled stems, 3-ranked leaves. Flowers in spikelets, perianth of bristles, achene fruit.
202. GRAMINEAE
Grasses, round hollow stems, 2-ranked leaves. Inflorescence spikelet, caryopsis fruit.
